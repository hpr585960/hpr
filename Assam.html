Assam





speciality of Assam



Assam is known for Assam tea and Assam silk. The state was the first site for oil drilling in Asia. Assam is home to the one-horned Indian rhinoceros, along with the wild water buffalo, pygmy hog, tiger and various species of Asiatic birds, and provides one of the last wild habitats for the Asian elephant.

Assam famous Tea & silk

**Assam Tea:** Assam is famous for its tea plantations and is one of the largest tea-producing regions in the world. The black tea produced here is known for its robust flavor and deep red color. 2. **Assam Silk:** The state is also known for its silk production, particularly Muga silk and Eri silk

Hill Stations

Haflong Hills, Diphu, Gangtok, Darjeeling, Mirik, Siliguri, Kalimpong, Kurseong, Jaldhaka, Rimbik, and Dooars and many more. The Hill Stations of Assam are comparatively unexplored and offer the best of the old world as well as modern charm.

Famous food

Khar. One of the most unique Assamese dishes that the locals love to devour on a regular basis is Khar. Masor Tenga. Pitika. Paror Mangxo. Haah Joha Kumura. Ou Khatta. Pani Hamuk. Baanhgajor Lagot Kukura.
